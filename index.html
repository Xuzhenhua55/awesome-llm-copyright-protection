In this era of AI advancement, protecting the intellectual property of language models has become crucial. Our project focuses on innovative techniques to safeguard these valuable AI assets.

In this project, we begin with a comprehensive preliminary section that introduces essential background knowledge for understanding various LLM IP protection methods. We provide updated definitions of key concepts such as model watermarking and model fingerprinting in the context of 2025, enabling newcomers to clearly distinguish between these often-confused concepts.

Moving forward, our model fingerprinting section systematically categorizes existing fingerprinting methods. For each category, we trace its evolution from traditional deep learning implementations to current LLM applications, offering insights into the origins and fundamental paradigms of these approaches.

We then explore an emerging direction: fingerprint transfer. This section examines transferable characteristics in invasive fingerprinting methods, followed by the latest developments in fingerprint detection and removal techniques.

Our ultimate goal is to present a comprehensive pipeline of copyright protection methods for large language models. From contemporary model fingerprint definitions to fingerprint embedding (and extraction) techniques, and from fingerprint transfer to removal strategies, we provide an end-to-end overview of the entire process.

This project encompasses various cutting-edge techniques including model fingerprinting and watermarking, transfer learning for copyright protection, and advanced methods for detecting and removing unauthorized model usage. Through these approaches, we aim to establish robust protection mechanisms for large language models in the AI era.
